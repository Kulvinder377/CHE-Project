Global CO2 Levels

Carbon dioxide (CO2) is a significant greenhouse gas that plays a crucial role in climate change. Over the past century, global CO2 levels have increased dramatically due to human activities, including burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. This rise in CO2 concentrations has led to an enhanced greenhouse effect, resulting in global warming and extreme weather events. Monitoring atmospheric CO2 levels is essential for understanding climate dynamics and assessing the effectiveness of mitigation strategies. The following graph illustrates the global average CO2 levels from 2000 to 2020, highlighting the trend of increasing concentrations and its implications for the environment.

Indian CO2 Levels

India is one of the largest emitters of carbon dioxide, primarily due to its reliance on coal for energy production, rapid industrialization, and urbanization. The increasing CO2 levels in India have significant consequences for air quality and public health, as well as contributing to global climate change. Urban areas experience much higher CO2 concentrations due to vehicular emissions and industrial activities. Monitoring CO2 levels is vital for developing effective policies aimed at reducing emissions and improving air quality. This section presents a graph that showcases the CO2 level trends in India from 2000 to 2020, emphasizing the urgency of addressing emissions in the country.
