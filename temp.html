Global Temperature Trends

Global temperature trends reflect the profound impact of climate change on the Earth’s environment. Over the last century, average temperatures have risen significantly, mainly due to human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. This increase has been accompanied by more frequent extreme weather events, rising sea levels, and disruptions to ecosystems. Understanding these trends is crucial for predicting future climate scenarios and developing effective strategies for mitigation and adaptation. The following graph illustrates the global average temperature rise from 2000 to 2020, highlighting key milestones in climate policy and natural events that have contributed to these changes.

Indian Temperature Trends

India, with its diverse climate zones, has experienced significant temperature fluctuations over the years. The Indian subcontinent has been warming at an alarming rate, impacting agriculture, water resources, and public health. Increased temperatures have led to severe droughts in some regions and flooding in others, disrupting the livelihoods of millions. In recent years, heatwaves have become more common, prompting concerns about heat-related illnesses and mortality. This section presents a detailed analysis of temperature trends in India, showcasing how climate change affects this densely populated country.
